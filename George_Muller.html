George Müller

The man of faith to whom God gave millons

A time line of George Müller's life:

  • 1805 -Born in Kroppenstaedt, Prussia -Now Germany
  • 1821 -Imprisoned at the age of 16 for 5 weeks
  • 1829 -Arrives in London as a Missionary
  • 1830 -Marries Mary, living in Teignmouth. Became Pastor of Ebenezer Chapel
  • 1832 -Moves to Bristol. For eight years he led two churches
  • 1834 -Establishes SKI - the Scriptural Knowledge Institute for Home and Abroad. The institution ran day schools and Sunday schools in Bristol but also supported missionaries overseas
    -Birth of his children, Elijah. George Müller and Mary had two children together, Lydia and Elijah. Unfortunately Elijah died of pneumonia in 1835
  • 1839 -By 1845 there were 4 houses on Wilson Street accommodating up to 130 orphans. When a polite letter was received from neighbours, outlining problems that the orphan houses were causing, such as noise levels and drains getting blocked, George decided to look at other options
    -1st Children's Home on Wilson Street, Bristol, during the cholera epidemic
  • 1849 -Move to Ashley Down. George decided to buy land to build dedicated houses for the orphans at Ashley Down on the outskirts of the city. The first home was completed in 1849 and housed 300 children
  • 1857 -2nd Ashley Down House Completed. It housed 400 children, taking the total to 700 so far
  • 1862 -3rd Ashley Down House Completed, housing up to 450 orphans
  • 1867-1870 -4th & 5th House. George decided he wanted to expand to house another 900 children. The fourth and fifth homes were completed between 1867 and 1870. In total the Ashley Down Orphanages housed 2050 orphans at one time
    -Mary's Death in February 1870. James Wright was appointed as assistant and successor to George Muller
  • 1871 -Muller remaries with Susannah Sangar and George's daughter Lydia married James Wright
  • 1875-1892 -George and his wife Travelled 200,000 miles side by side as missionaries across 42 countries
  • 1898 -George Müller died on the 10th March at the age of 92. On the day of his funeral so many people came out to pay their respects that Bristol came to a standstill
“Money is really worth no more than as it can be used to accomplish the Lord's work. Life is worth as much as it is spent for the Lord's service.”

--George Müller, The Autobiography of George Müller
